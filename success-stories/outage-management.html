The Challenge

Our client, a regional utility company serving over 2 million customers across three states, faced a purely reactive approach to outage detection, relying on customer calls after disruptions. This resulted in average detection times of 35 minutes and a mean restoration time of nearly 4 hours, both well above industry benchmarks. Customer satisfaction scores were 17% below average, primarily due to poor communication and slow response handling.

Our Solution: Integrated Predictive Data

We developed an integrated platform combining LSTM neural networks for temporal pattern analysis with real-time GIS mapping. The system integrates weather data, equipment maintenance history, and 12,500+ smart IoT sensors across the distribution network to identify threats before they result in service loss.

Critical Features

Key features include Predictive Storm Impact Modeling which identifies grid vulnerability and high-risk areas prior to weather events. The cause classification moduleDetermines likely outage reasons using automated vegetation risk assessment via satellite and drone imagery, allowing for first-time resolution by optimized field crews.

Project Conclusion

This implementation transformed the utility provider from a reactive model to a data-driven, proactive approach. Beyond immediate operational efficiency and $5.3 million in annual savings, the company improved its J.D. Power customer satisfaction scores by 24% while exceeding all reliability targets for the first time in company history.
